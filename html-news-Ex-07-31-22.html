Crews battling the McKinney Fire in northern California were bracing for thunderstorms and hot, windy conditions that created the potential for additional fire growth Sunday. The blaze exploded in size to more than 80 square miles just two days after erupting in a largely unpopulated area of Siskiyou County.The blaze torched trees along California Highway 96, and the scorched remains of a pickup truck sat in a lane of the highway.
